Methodology

How a grade is built.

Public record first

Voting records, sponsored bills, committee testimony, and floor statements weigh more than survey answers. Past behavior predicts future behavior.

Written survey second

A standardized questionnaire on each pillar. Open answers, not just yes/no. Refusals are public.

Cross-checked third

Statements verified against the General Court roll-call record and primary documents. We don't grade what a candidate says — we grade what they do.

Published, period

Every grade and the underlying evidence are public. No private endorsements. No "we'll quietly support X."
